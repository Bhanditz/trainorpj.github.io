My name is PJ Trainor. I'm an analyst at the Johns Hopkins University Applied Physics Lab. I like javascript, film, and jokes about animals.

My journey in coding started out making data visualizations, but now I'm involved in all sorts of front-end open source software — Vue, (P)React, d3, babel, build tools, and more.

Learning is important to me. I'm self-taught in a lot of things, so I know how hard it is to get started. To help others, I write. Whenever I learned something new or challenging, I try to write about it. Sometimes it's about code, but I also like to write about film and math.

I'm currently working towards my Master's in Applied Math. I went to Swarthmore College for undergrad, where I studied Math, Film, and Economics.
